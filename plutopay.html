Pluto Pay

Why Pluto pay?

Through this app, users will be informed whether their set budget for their spending or saving is approaching. This way users will be in control of their finance and meet their spending/saving goals

Skills employed

I used the guidelines from Material Design system to design this application. I also conducted user interviews, user testing, and competitive analysis for this project.

Tools

  • Figma
  • Marvel

Problem Statement

User is looking for a digital payment solution that allows them to set a budget for their spending and receive notifications when they are approaching or exceeding that budget. While feeling safe when making transaction on the application

Solution

Pluto pay's concept is a combination of two applications. One is a digital payment application, and the other is a budgeting application. The main idea is to create an app where users can send payments within their social network while managing their finances. The application allows users to connect multiple bank accounts and credit cards, providing an easy way to view their spending.

User testing, feedback, and design adjustment

Home Screen

Feedback: The boxes used for pay again and transaction history seem a bit packed together. Does the information ends at the bottom, or do I need to click the see all CTA button to view more?

Improvement: I used the tab design style from the design material to combine the two elements. I was hoping to give off a less cluttered aesthetic. Removing the box surrounding the element will show the users that it s scrollable. I kept the see all CTA button for the user to view its entirety if they want to.

Confrimation Screen

Feedback: The confirmation screen feels excellent and intuitive. But the message and the CTA button seem a bit far apart.

Improvement: I agree with the comment. The law of proximity says that " how the human eye perceives elements that are close together as more related than elements that are further apart." I put the confirmation message closer to the middle of the screen. Showcasing that is the most important message for that page and being proportion with the rest of the elements

Pay recipient page

Feedback: The two action buttons are identical for the payment recipient page.

Improvement: I agree with the comment. The CTA buttons look identical and can easily confuse users. I added a new color that complimented my primary color and redesigned the buttons to a flat rectangle instead of the 3D button for visual clarity.
